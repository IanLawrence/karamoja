SITUATIONAL OVERVIEW

The district still remains in ALARM because of the prevailing hunger gap, which has resulted from the depletion of household food stocks. The crop sector is in ALERT because of the low acreages of land cultivated this year, which is not enough to sustain the households until the next planting season, and is expected to cause another hunger gap next year. The livestock sector remains in ALERT due to the prevalence of diseases such as Trypanosomiasis, CBPP, CCPP and common tick borne diseases like anoplasmosis and ECF across the district. However, improvement is expected since the district veterinary office is carrying out mass treatment and plans to vaccinate cattle and shoats against CBPP and CCPP across the three livelihood zones.

The water sector remains Normal and it is expected to be stable. The livelihood sector remains in ALARM because of the hunger gap, which is still prominent, high prevalence of malnutrition and the low price of casual labour across the three livelihood zones. Most foods consumed in the district are still being purchased from the market and others from food relief being provided by WFP, World Vision and Office of the Prime Minister to the most vulnerable populations. This trend is expected to continue since the first harvest is not expected until late September 2013.

For recommendations refer to page 2 below.

The data presented in this Drought Bulletin was collected in 8 sampled parishes from Karenga, Lobalangit, Lolelia, Kalapata, Kaabong East & West, Sidok, Kapedo and Loyoro sub-counties, Kaabong District.
